The Rheinfall in Dachsen, Switzerland, is one of Europe's largest and most powerful waterfalls. Located near the German border in the Canton of Zurich, this stunning natural feature spans 150 meters wide and drops 23 meters. Formed during the last ice age, about 14,000 to 17,000 years ago, the Rheinfall combines breathtaking natural beauty with significant geological history. Easily accessible for tourists, it offers boat tours, scenic walking trails, and viewing platforms.
